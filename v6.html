About
Alina Abraham
UGA Student  ·  Mental Health Advocate  ·  Crisis Textline Volunteer

I've spent the last year talking to therapists, clinics, and people who quit therapy. Not because therapy failed them, but because the system made it too hard to stick with. The weeks between appointments, the sessions that felt like starting over — there's a gap there that nobody is filling.

This check-in is my attempt to fill it. Just 2 minutes a night, and something to hold onto between sessions.

I'm a UGA student testing whether this idea is real. If it helps you, it helps me know whether to keep building.
