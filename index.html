The 2-Week Check-In — In Detail

Three forms. Zero fluff.

Each form is short on purpose. The goal is consistency, not perfection.

Form 01 · One time

Getting Started

Filled out before Day 1 so I understand where you're starting from. Takes about 5 minutes.

  • What are you currently working on in therapy?
  • What does a "good week" look like for you?
  • What does a "hard week" look like for you?
  • What would make this 2 weeks worth it for you?
Form 02 · Every night at 8pm

Daily Check-In

A quick pulse check on how you're doing. Takes less than 2 minutes.

  • How are you feeling today? (1–10 scale)
  • What's been on your mind the most?
  • Anything you'd want to tell your therapist?
  • Did you use anything from your last session?
  • What do you need most right now?
Form 03 · Day 7 and Day 14

Weekly Reflection

Zoom out and look at the bigger picture. Your answers anchor your progress summary. Takes about 3 minutes.

  • Compared to last week, how are you feeling?
  • One thing you noticed about yourself this week
  • One thing to focus on going into next week
  • How useful has this check-in been? (1–5 scale)
About
Alina Abraham
UGA Student  ·  Mental Health Advocate  ·  Crisis Textline Volunteer

I've spent the last year talking to therapists, clinics, and people who quit therapy. Not because therapy failed them, but because the system made it too hard to stick with. The wrong fit, the sessions that felt like starting over, the weeks between appointments with nothing to hold onto.

These two services are my attempt to change that. Not with an app. Not with a platform. Just with real attention, a consistent structure, and the belief that showing up for yourself, even for 2 minutes a day, is worth something.

I'm a UGA student testing whether this idea is real. If it helps you, it helps me know whether to keep building.
